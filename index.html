1. Problem Statement & Scope

Extreme Wet Bulb Temperatures pose severe risks to human survivability. Our goal in Track 1 is to accurately predict these critical limits.

  • Analyzing vast spatiotemporal dependencies.
  • Processing complex meteorological metrics.
  • Building a robust predictive foundation for extreme weather.

2. Data Pipeline & Preprocessing

High-quality predictions require immaculate data engineering. Here is how we processed the 41-year NASA dataset.

  • Handled missing values and temporal gaps seamlessly.
  • Engineered geographical features (latitude/longitude mapping).
  • Normalized thermal and atmospheric pressure variants.

3. Feature Engineering

We created targeted features that capture physical interactions within the highly volatile climate system.

  • Calculated Air-to-Soil temperature gradients.
  • Modeled Evaporation flux and soil wetness ratios.
  • Applied Cyclic time encoding to preserve year-over-year seasonality.

4. Model Architecture

Our approach utilizes an optimized gradient boosting ensemble for maximum accuracy, speed, and stability.

  • LightGBM fine-tuned specifically for Mean Absolute Error (MAE).
  • GroupKFold validation implemented to prevent geographical data leakage.
  • Engineered for high efficiency on massive spatiotemporal datasets.
